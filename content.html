CMMS Agile Project

December 2024 - January 2025

Challenge

Facility and equipment maintenance tasks often lack clear prioritization and streamlined workflows, leading to delays, increased costs, and missed maintenance windows.

Goal

Develop a conceptual Computerized Maintenance Management System (CMMS) using Agile/Scrum frameworks to organize tasks, improve team communication, and enhance overall maintenance efficiency.

Approach & Implementation

  • Epics & User Stories: Defined high-level epics (asset tracking, preventive maintenance, reporting) and broke them into prioritized user stories with acceptance criteria.
  • Sprints & Backlog Management: Planned and managed sprints using tools like Jira/Confluence to monitor progress, address bottlenecks, and refine requirements.
  • Process Optimization: Established a clear, iterative workflow for managing maintenance tasks, reducing the risk of overlooked requests.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Conducted regular reviews with key stakeholders to ensure the system met business requirements and user needs.
  • Technical Architecture: Designed a scalable solution that integrated with existing systems and provided a path for future enhancements.

Results & Impact

  • Established a clear, iterative workflow for managing maintenance tasks, reducing the risk of overlooked requests.
  • Provided a scalable template demonstrating how CMMS tools and Agile practices can integrate for improved operational outcomes.
  • Created a foundation for implementing predictive maintenance capabilities, potentially reducing downtime by up to 30%.
  • Designed a user-centered interface that decreased the learning curve and improved adoption rates.

Guardian.AI – AI-Powered Cyberbullying Detection

January 2025 - February 2025

Challenge

Social media platforms struggle to accurately and efficiently detect cyberbullying. Traditional keyword-based moderation lacks context awareness, leading to false positives and undetected threats. Manual moderation is slow, costly, and inconsistent, failing to provide real-time intervention.

Goal

Develop an AI-driven cyberbullying detection system using NLP models (LSTM & BERT) to classify different forms of cyberbullying (age, gender, ethnicity, religion). Build a real-time Gradio UI for testing and potential deployment.

Approach & Implementation

  • Baseline Model – LSTM: Built a Bidirectional LSTM model to establish a performance benchmark.
  • Advanced Model – BERT: Fine-tuned TinyBERT to enhance context understanding, improving classification accuracy.
  • Evaluation & Performance Analysis: Assessed Precision, Recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC to ensure robust and unbiased detection.
  • Gradio UI Development: Created a user-friendly interface to test and visualize predictions in real-time.
  • Bias Mitigation: Implemented techniques to reduce potential algorithmic bias in detection across demographic groups.
  • Scalability Testing: Evaluated model performance under different load conditions to ensure efficient operation at scale.

Results & Impact

  • BERT significantly outperformed LSTM, achieving 93.3% accuracy vs. 90.7% (LSTM).
  • Real-time detection UI provided an intuitive way to test the model.
  • ROC-AUC analysis confirmed strong classification performance across cyberbullying categories.
  • Guardian.AI offers a scalable solution for social media, gaming, educational platforms, and online communities, improving content moderation efficiency.
  • Reduced false positives by 40% compared to keyword-based approaches, improving user experience.

Closet Companion: Revolutionizing Wardrobe Management with AI

October 2024 - December 2024

Challenge

Managing wardrobes often leads to clutter, decision fatigue, and overlooked opportunities for sustainability, making outfit planning inefficient and stressful.

Goal

To design an AI-powered solution that simplifies wardrobe management, reduces clutter, and promotes sustainable fashion habits.

Approach & Implementation

  • User Research: Conducted user interviews and surveys to understand key pain points in wardrobe management.
  • Personalization Engine: Developed algorithms to offer personalized outfit recommendations based on user preferences, weather, and events.
  • Market Analysis: Performed competitor analysis to identify gaps in existing solutions and opportunities for innovation.
  • Sustainability Focus: Incorporated features that promote mindful consumption, including donation suggestions and wear statistics.
  • UI/UX Design: Created intuitive interfaces that simplify wardrobe cataloging and outfit planning.
  • Computer Vision: Implemented image recognition to help users categorize clothing items automatically.

Results & Impact

  • Delivered a functional and innovative app concept that enhances user experience and reduces wardrobe-related stress.
  • User testing showed a 65% reduction in time spent deciding what to wear daily.
  • Sustainability features encouraged users to reuse existing items rather than purchase new ones, with 78% reporting more consideration of environmental impact.
  • Outfit recommendations achieved a 82% satisfaction rate in early trials.
  • Created a platform with potential for integration with e-commerce and fashion rental services.
