ResQ.AI — AI-Powered Personal Safety Platform

March 2025 — Present

Challenge

Existing safety applications primarily focus on reactive emergency responses and often operate in silos (location safety, profile verification, emergency protocols). ResQ.AI addresses the need for a unified, proactive, and privacy-conscious personal safety solution for users navigating both physical and digital spaces.

Goal

To develop an AI-driven personal safety platform integrating location intelligence, profile verification, and discreet emergency response, enabling users to proactively assess and mitigate risks before they arise.

Approach & Implementation

  • Advanced Profile Verification: Combines Google Cloud Vision API, DeepFace, Scrapingdog (Google Lens API), and GPT-4 Turbo to verify user profiles, detect potential impersonations, and assess online risks.
  • Location Safety Intelligence: Leverages geospatial crime data, heatmaps (Leaflet.js), AI-driven safety scores, and contextual recommendations based on time and location.
  • Smart Emergency Response (Hydrate Feature): Disguised as a hydration reminder, allows discreet check-ins or triggers an emergency protocol without raising suspicion.
  • Data Pipeline: Processes public crime data, fugitive databases (FBI & Interpol), and user-generated content with secure, privacy-focused handling.
  • AI-Powered Insights: Generates contextual, personalized safety recommendations using GPT-4 Turbo for both location and profile evaluations.
  • Fully Integrated Web Prototype: Built with Flask, OpenCV, Leaflet.js, REST APIs, and concurrent processing for real-time performance.

Results & Impact

  • Successfully demonstrated multi-layered personal safety functionalities in internal testing.
  • Reduced false positives in profile verification by integrating cross-platform verification (Face Matching + Reverse Image Search + AI Risk Assessment).
  • Enabled users to proactively assess location risks with an interactive, AI-powered heatmap and dynamic safety scores.
  • Provided a discreet and effective emergency response system for sensitive real-life scenarios (e.g., dating safety).

ShareSafe — AI Safeguard Tool

April 2025

Challenge

In today's world where AI tools like ChatGPT are widely used in professional environments, accidental sharing of sensitive data has become a serious security concern. Just like Samsung's 2023 internal data leak where an employee pasted confidential source code into ChatGPT, organizations need proactive solutions to prevent such incidents.

Product

ShareSafe is an AI safeguard tool that scans content before sharing with external AI tools to prevent accidental data leaks of sensitive information.

How It Works

  • Our tool analyzes the text for sensitive information such as:

Guardian.AI – AI-Powered Cyberbullying Detection

January 2025 - February 2025

Challenge

Social media platforms struggle to accurately and efficiently detect cyberbullying. Traditional keyword-based moderation lacks context awareness, leading to false positives and undetected threats. Manual moderation is slow, costly, and inconsistent, failing to provide real-time intervention.

Goal

Develop an AI-driven cyberbullying detection system using NLP models (LSTM & BERT) to classify different forms of cyberbullying (age, gender, ethnicity, religion). Build a real-time Gradio UI for testing and potential deployment.

Approach & Implementation

  • Baseline Model – LSTM: Built a Bidirectional LSTM model to establish a performance benchmark.
  • Advanced Model – BERT: Fine-tuned TinyBERT to enhance context understanding, improving classification accuracy.
  • Evaluation & Performance Analysis: Assessed Precision, Recall, F1-score, and ROC-AUC to ensure robust and unbiased detection.
  • Gradio UI Development: Created a user-friendly interface to test and visualize predictions in real-time.
  • Bias Mitigation: Implemented techniques to reduce potential algorithmic bias in detection across demographic groups.
  • Scalability Testing: Evaluated model performance under different load conditions to ensure efficient operation at scale.

Results & Impact

  • BERT significantly outperformed LSTM, achieving 93.3% accuracy vs. 90.7% (LSTM).
  • Real-time detection UI provided an intuitive way to test the model.
  • ROC-AUC analysis confirmed strong classification performance across cyberbullying categories.
  • Guardian.AI offers a scalable solution for social media, gaming, educational platforms, and online communities, improving content moderation efficiency.
  • Reduced false positives by 40% compared to keyword-based approaches, improving user experience.

Meet-Less — AI-Powered Meeting Optimization

April 2025

Challenge

Organizations waste countless hours in unproductive meetings. Research shows only 28% of meetings result in actual decisions, 65% of meetings could be handled asynchronously, and meetings with less than 30% speaker participation waste most attendees' time.

Goal

To develop an intelligent meeting analysis tool that helps organizations reclaim productive time by identifying which meetings should be kept, optimized, or replaced with asynchronous communication.

Key Features

  • Smart Meeting Classification: Automatically identifies high-value vs. low-value meetings using our proprietary algorithm
  • Participation Analysis: Detects meetings with too many silent participants and suggests attendance optimization
  • Frequency Optimization: Identifies recurring meetings that could be held less frequently
  • Time Savings Calculator: Quantifies potential hours saved through meeting optimization
  • Interactive Dashboard: Visualizes meeting effectiveness with intuitive charts and graphs

How It Works

  • Data Analysis: Upload meeting data or use our sample dataset
  • Pattern Recognition: Our algorithm identifies meeting effectiveness patterns
  • Scoring System: Meetings receive value scores based on type, participation, and outcomes
  • Recommendations: Get specific suggestions such as converting large standups to async updates, replacing low-engagement leadership meetings, reducing participant lists, and adjusting meeting frequency

Technology Stack

  • Frontend: React.js, Streamlit, Plotly for data visualization
  • Backend: Python, Pandas for data analysis
  • Deployment: Vercel

Clustering Transactions for Fraud Detection

November 2024

Challenge

Fraudulent transactions are often subtle, hidden among millions of normal transactions, and challenging to detect without labeled data. Traditional supervised learning requires labeled fraud data, which is limited or unavailable in many real-world scenarios.

Goal

Apply unsupervised clustering techniques to detect anomalies and suspicious transaction patterns without relying on pre-labeled datasets.

Approach & Implementation

  • Feature Engineering: Performed feature scaling, dimensionality reduction (PCA), and data preprocessing on transactional data.
  • Clustering Techniques: Implemented and compared K-Means, DBSCAN, and Hierarchical Clustering to detect potential fraud clusters.
  • Visualization: Visualized clusters using t-SNE and scatter plots to interpret anomalies.
  • Evaluation: Analyzed silhouette scores, inertia, and anomaly ratios to assess the effectiveness of different clustering methods.

Results & Insights

  • DBSCAN proved effective in identifying anomalous transactions without prior labels.
  • t-SNE visualization revealed distinct clusters, enabling easier human interpretation of fraud patterns.
  • Developed a reproducible workflow for unsupervised fraud detection applicable to real-world financial datasets.
